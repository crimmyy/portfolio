Excited to meet you!

I’m Aidan, an artist/designer passionate about creative technologies, whether this be UI, products, or interactive media.

I'm currently a research assistant at the Human-Computer Interaction Institute at Carnegie Mellon University, where I am also a junior double majoring in Fine Arts and Human-Computer Interaction.

I grew up in Atlanta, Georgia in a family deeply rooted in the arts and technology—my mother is a graphic designer, and my father is a software engineer. This blend of influences has shaped my approach to both my studies and my personal projects.

At Carnegie Mellon, I have received recognition for my commitment to my craft. These include the Regina Gouger Miller and Marlin Miller, Jr. Scholarship for undergraduate studies in art, and the Samuel Rosenberg Award in consecutive years, recognizing my talents as a first and second-year.

When I'm not busy with research/design/studio work, I find solace in music and running. Whether it’s a quiet jog through Schenley Park or losing myself in music, these activities help me maintain myself.
